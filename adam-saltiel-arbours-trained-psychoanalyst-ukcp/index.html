The Psycho-Analysis Clinic

Group and Individual Supervision

for Counsellors and Psychotherapists

Adam Saltiel (Arbours Trained Psychoanalyst, UKCP)

Julia Saltiel (PsychodynamicPsychotherapist, Art Therapist)

We have limited places in three supervision groups which have been organised as male only, female only and both male and female.

In the near future, we will also offer a multi-disciplinary supervision group.

For a variety of reasons many men and women will welcome the opportunity to explore the detailed issues of therapeutic work in single sex groups.

Others will prefer the opportunity to work with those from the opposite sex while examining issues appropriate for therapeutic supervision.

Each of these supervision groups is limited to six people only.

We wish to encourage students and practicing psychotherapists in growing their understanding of psychotherapy.

By offering the opportunity for detailed discussion of case work, listening carefully to one another and exploring each others reactions we will provide an unequaled learning environment. One that is not based on competitiveness or intellectual assertiveness and which allows each individual practitioner their own space and pace to grow.

Drawing on our wealth of experience and knowledge we enable our fellow practitioners to find their own insights into the work they embark on.

At the same time there is always a place for the insights afforded by theory.

Weaving carefully between the two areas our frontiers of understanding will expand together without losing our way.

Below summarises the individual practice experience we have.

Experience in individual, couple, family and community work with a wide range of difficulties.

As a representative illustration, this encompasses sexual and physical addictions (sex as a substitute, physical substance abuse as a substitute for meaningful contact), obsessive neurotic anxieties as well as childhood deprivation, depression, sexual abuse and traumatic stress. This includes very dangerous to self and disturbing conditions such as self harm, anorexia and different levels of personality disorder and psychosis.

We work with all ages (with children together with their parents, older adolescents individually) including individuals with disabilities and families who may have a disabled member.

In our clinical practice, supervision and referral service we model collaboration between professionals.

We work with people who are on medication and receiving care from a team.

Responsible liaising with GPs, across medical teams and with consultants is a priority for us.

Similarly, we also liaise with other members of an individual's community such as key workers and Rabbis or representative.
